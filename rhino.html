The Rhino

A Prehistoric Powerhouse

An Armored Giant

The rhinoceros is a living relic, a heavyweight survivor from an ancient lineage. Their most notable feature is the formidable horn, which is not bone, but compacted keratin. Their thick, plate-like skin gives them an armored appearance, a defense mechanism perfected over millions of years.

A Surprising Turn of Speed

Despite their immense size and bulk, rhinos are deceptively fast. When charging, a black rhino can reach speeds of up to 55 km/h (34 mph), using their momentum and powerful horn as a devastating weapon against any perceived threat. This combination of size, armor, and speed makes them one of the most respected and feared herbivores in Africa.
